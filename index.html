Research

Muon g-2 Collaboration
University of Washington
Fermilab
2017-Present
  • Member of the Muon g-2 Collaboration, which is currently seeking to measure the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon (aμ) to 140 parts per billion.
  • Co-Detector Coordinator: 2019-Present. In charge of maintaining the operation of detector subsystems during data taking.
  • Nearline Manager: Currently managing the 'Nearline' data processing system, which automatically processes approximately 30% of the data taken by the experiment within 2 hours of it being taken. This serves as an input for many systematic studies for which fully verified data quality control is not necessary.
Deshpande Group
Stony Brook University
2014-2017
  • Designed an Electromagnetic Calorimeter for implementation at BNL in scheduled EIC upgrade under Dr. Abhay Deshpande and Dr. Nils Feege. Used this detector simulation to search for Leptoquark production in electron-proton collisions.
  • Aided in the design and testing of a magnetic field cloaking device for use in the proposed EIC.
  • Traveled to Argonne National Lab and Brookhaven National Lab to perform measurement of prototype magnetic field cloaking device.
SUNY Plattsburgh
2012-2013
  • Studied the plasmonic behaviour of Nickel-Iron thin films under Dr. Ken Podolak.
